Tenacity privacy policy


Last modified: May 1, 2016

Tenacity’s mission is to close college information gaps facing underserved communities (i.e. low income and first generation students). Tenacity uses online tools and content to ensure that everyone has the confidence and know-how to pursue higher education or to support others in doing so. We recognize the importance of protecting the privacy rights of our students and mentors (“users”).

This Privacy Policy discloses privacy practices for www.mytenacity.org (“Site”), Tenacity’s mobile text message feature, and Tenacity’s mentorship component (all three collectively “the Service”). This Privacy Policy applies solely to information collected by the Service.

Information We Collect and How We Use It

General
Tenacity uses information collected by the Service consistent with our mission.

  1. We may sell and/or share certain data to higher education institutions only from users who have opted to be contacted by third-party organizations. Such data may include extracurricular activities student users have tracked and identified as participating in or schools that student users have on their college lists.
  2. We may de-identify and/or aggregate information that users provide to conduct research in collaboration with higher education institutions.

Basic Account Information
When students or mentors create an online user account, we collect personally identifiable information, including but not limited to: first name, last name, grade level, email address, password, and phone number (optional). We use users’ telephone numbers only to send weekly text message reminders. Users may opt-out of this feature at any time.

  1. Users’ phone numbers are shared with Twilio, a company which administers the text message functionality. Twilio stores the phone numbers and SMS logs securely. Tenacity will not otherwise share the contents of text messages.

Additional Information
When the student user completes tasks on the online portal, we may also collect a wide variety of academic, extracurricular, financial, and personal information as it pertains to the college preparation process. This information is subject to change at any time as we revise and improve our content.

Pilot Programs
Some individuals may be invited to participate in pilot programs in collaboration with community organizations or educational institutions. If your user account is created during a pilot program, the community organizer, counselor, point of contact, or leader in charge of the program will automatically be given access to your account. If you wish to revoke this third-party’s access to your account, you must forfeit participation in the pilot program.

Log Data
When you use our Site, we may receive information (“Log Data”) such as your IP address, browser type, operating system, the referring web page, pages visited, location, your mobile carrier, device information (including device and application IDs), search terms, and cookie information. We receive Log Data when you interact with our Service, for example, when you visit our Site, sign into our Site, interact with our email notifications and text messages, or use your account to authenticate to a third-party website or application. We may also receive Log Data when you click on, view, or interact with links on our Site, including links to third-party applications, such as the FAFSA. Tenacity uses Log Data to provide, understand, and improve our Site. We store Log Data security and indefinitely. We have a strict policy of not storing any identifying or sensitive information in the logs.

Cookies
We use “cookies” on this Site. A cookie is a piece of data stored on a Site visitor's hard drive to help us improve your access to our Site and identify repeat visitors to our Site. For instance, when we use a cookie to identify you, you would not have to log in a password more than once, thereby saving time while on our Site. Cookies can also enable us to track and target the interests of our users to enhance their experience on our Site. Usage of a cookie is in no way linked to any personally identifiable information on our Site. You can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level. If you turn off your cookie settings, you may still use our Site, but your ability to use some features or areas of the Service may be limited.

Your Access to and Control Over Information

You may opt out of any future contacts from us at any time. You can do the following at any time by contacting us via the email address or phone number given on our Site:

  1. See what data we have about you, if any;
  2. Change or correct any data we have about you;
  3. Have us delete any data we have about you, including deleting your account; and
  4. Express any concern you have about our use of your data.

Use by Children Under 13 Years of Age is Prohibited

The Service is not intended for use by persons under 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information online from children under the age of 13. If you become aware that your child, or any child, has provided us with their personal information, please contact us [here] to have the user account terminated. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to remove such information and terminate the child’s account.

Third-Party Links

This Site contains links to other websites. Please be aware that we are not responsible or liable for the content or privacy practices of such other websites. We encourage our users to be aware when they leave our Site and to read the privacy statements of any other website that collects personally identifiable information. Nevertheless, we seek to protect the integrity of our Site and welcome any feedback about these linked websites, including if a specific link does not work.

Information Security

We work hard to protect Tenacity and our users from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of information we hold. In particular:

  1. We encrypt our Site using state-of-the-art TLS 1.2 encryption.
  2. We review our information collection, storage, and processing practices, including physical security measures, to guard against unauthorized access to systems.
  3. We restrict access to personal information to Tenacity LLC members, employees, contractors, and agents who need to know that information in order to process it for us, and who are subject to strict contractual confidentiality obligations and may be disciplined or terminated if they fail to meet these obligations.

Changes to Our Privacy Policy

Our Privacy Policy may change from time to time, and all updates will be posted on this page. Policy changes will apply only to information collected after the date of the change, unless otherwise specified.
